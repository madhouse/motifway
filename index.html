Motif on Wayland

Wayland is the future of the GNU/Linux desktop, more and more widget sets, libraries and programs are ported to it. Motif has been - and on some platforms, still is - the main widget toolkit, with faithful followers and fans all around the globe. To preserve the legacy that is Motif, it is only fitting, if it gets ported to Wayland too.

Goals

Open Source

The library will be available under the same GNU Lesser General Public License as the original Motif.

Compatible

The headers and the API will remain 100% compatible: if it compiled with Motif, it will compile with the ported library too.

Modern

We will use modern tools and methodologies during development, to guarantee the library's stability.

Campaign

Why?
With modern toolkits around, porting Motif may, indeed, sound futile. But think about the Legacy we preserve! The ability to run that old program you found at the bottom of a drawer, which brings sentimental memories to the surface! That, dear reader, is the motivation for this project. And with Your help, we can do it!

Who?
Having contributed to a lot of strange things in my life (ranging from the ratpoison window manager, through syslog-ng to Hy), my passion is to take on tasks that may seem strange, irrelevant or useless.

How?
Since I have a day job, in order to be able to spend enough time on the library, I will need funding that more or less matches my salary. That way, I can take a month or two off from work (depending on how much is raised), and dedicate my time to this project. Would the campaign fail to reach the target goal, the library will still be made, but I will not be able to temporarily suspend my day job, therefore the speed of development will be so much slower.

What for?
To facilitate the funding of the campaign, the perks were chosen with the goal of appealing to people like me, who take pride in keeping things alive: you can sponsor commits, or even functions, and if you are - like me - a big fan of physical memorabilia, you can even choose to receive a dedicated USB stick with the full source code of the finished product, and binary packages for the then-stable Debian GNU/Linux release and the most recent Ubuntu LTS.

How can I help?
Any help with this campaign is appreciated, not just funding! If you can tweet, share, tell your friends about the campaign, that is also a big help! Screenshots of old Motif environments for the campaign, or even screencasts about Motif are also of tremendous value. If you have, or want to contribute such, do not hesitate to contact us!
